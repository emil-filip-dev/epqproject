Explanation Page 2: Nuclear fusion

Nuclear fusion is what happens when two atoms "fuse" together into a single, larger atom.

This new atom tends to have more protons and neutrons than the other, smaller atoms. However, sometimes, a neutron or even larger particles may be emitted when the two atoms fuse.

Nuclear fusion can happen with any element on the periodic table, and their isotopes. However, it is easier to achieve with smaller atoms than larger ones.

For this reason, the main nuclear fusion reaction worth considering is that involving deuterium and tritium:

This reaction produces a free neutron, a helium nucleus and energy.

The energy produced may seem like it comes from nowhere, but that is not the case. The resultant mass of the reaction is smaller than the input mass.

The change in mass gets converted to energy according to E = mc2.

Fusion produces a lot of energy, but not infinite energy.
