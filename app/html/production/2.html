Production Page 2: Stellarators

A stellarator is similar to a tokamak, except that external magnets are used instead of letting the plasma act as its own magnet.

These magnets are essentially "coils" that are built around a torus-shaped cloud of plasma. They conduct a current and generate a magnetic field around them that affects the plasma as well.

Some stellarator designs may be twisted so as to better contain the plasma.

Stellarators do not use an electric current to heat and contain the plasma.

Instead, they use something called a magnetic pumping system. Radio waves are sent into the plasma chamber, energising the plasma particles and making them flow closer together, resulting in an increase in pressure.

This increase in pressure also results in an increase in temperature.

Thus, the entire plasma cloud does not move and is not charged (even though at the atomic level, nuclei are moving around at a high speed).
