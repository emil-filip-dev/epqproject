Production Page 1: Tokamaks

A tokamak is a machine used to produce electricity using fusion.

A tokamak works by taking in fuel and heating it up to a very high temperature until it turns into plasma. It then makes the plasma flow in a torus shape (a doughnut shape).

The flowing plasma then acts as its own magnet, keeping itself together as fusion occurs.

This requires an electric current to be run through the plasma. Running an electric current through anything will result in a magnetic field being produced as well, which is the way the plasma is contained.

The transformer at the centre of the torus shape in the picture above is where the electricity will be flowing from.

The tokamak is considered the most viable candidate for a conceivable fusion energy device, and hence research into a stable design is actively receiving the most funding from organisations.
