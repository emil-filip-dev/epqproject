Research Page 3: Wendelstein 7-X

This is the most well-known example of stellarator technology being used.

The Wendelstein 7-X was completed in October 2015, in Greifswald, Germany.

Its purpose is to provide a way of researching possible improvements to the stellarator design, so it does not produce electricity. Any heat it produces is released into the atmosphere.

The W7-X functions as a typical stellarator.

Its coils produce a very strong magnetic field to contain the plasma, the plasma is heated and pressurized until it fuses.

Supercomputers were involved in the design stage of the W7-X.

This could pave a way for computer-managed fusion reactions, which could potentially be much more efficient.
